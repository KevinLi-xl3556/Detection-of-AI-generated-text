Jack Van Impe, End Times Preacher on TV, Is Dead at 88
The end has come for Jack Van Impe, the televangelist who spent decades preaching about the end times, predicting the arrival of the Antichrist, and offering his views on biblical prophecy. He passed away on January 18, 2020, at the age of 88.
Van Impe began his career in the early 1950s as a traveling preacher before becoming a fixture on television in the 1980s. He hosted a weekly program titled Jack Van Impe Presents, which aired on various networks including TBN, Daystar, and the Christian Broadcasting Network. He was known for his distinctive baritone voice and flashy suits, and for providing a biblical interpretation of current events.
One of Van Impe’s primary focuses was on the end times, which he believed were imminent. He frequently cited the Book of Revelation and other biblical passages to predict the arrival of the Antichrist, the Rapture, and other events. He also issued warnings about the dangers of various world religions, including Islam and Eastern spirituality.
Van Impe faced criticism from some corners for his apocalyptic worldview, which some saw as fear-mongering or even dangerous. However, he maintained that his message was one of hope and salvation, urging viewers to turn to Jesus in order to be saved from the coming tribulations.
Despite his controversial views, Van Impe maintained a loyal following throughout his career. He was known for his tireless work ethic, often producing dozens of episodes of his show each year. He was also a prolific writer, authoring numerous books on biblical prophecy and other topics.
Van Impe’s passing marks the end of an era for the world of televangelism. Though his message may have been divisive, his impact on the genre cannot be denied. He leaves behind a legacy of preaching, scholarship, and passionate belief.
In the end, whether or not Van Impe’s predictions come to pass remains to be seen. What is clear, however, is that his influence on the world of religion will be felt for years to come. Whether viewed as a prophet or a teacher, his legacy will continue to inspire and challenge those who grapple with the mysteries of faith and the unknown future.
