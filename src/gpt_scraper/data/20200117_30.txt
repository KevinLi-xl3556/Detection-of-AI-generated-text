Ninez Cacho-Olivares, Journalist and Critic of Marcos, Dies at 78
The Philippine media industry mourns the loss of one of its most prominent and courageous journalists, Ninez Cacho-Olivares, who passed away at the age of 78. Cacho-Olivares was known for her unwavering commitment to truth and justice, as well as her fearless critique of the Marcos regime during the dark days of martial law in the Philippines.
Born on June 28, 1944, in Manila, Cacho-Olivares began her career in journalism at a young age. She worked for various newspapers and publications, including the Philippine Daily Express and the Manila Times, where she earned a reputation as a tenacious and uncompromising reporter. Her sharp insights and unrelenting pursuit of the truth earned her the respect of her peers and the admiration of readers.
However, it was during the oppressive regime of Ferdinand Marcos that Cacho-Olivares truly distinguished herself as a journalist of great integrity and courage. In the face of censorship and threats to her safety, she fearlessly exposed the human rights abuses and corruption that were rampant during the Marcos era. Her incisive reporting and fearless criticism of the government earned her the ire of the Marcos regime, and she became a target of harassment and intimidation.
Despite the risks, Cacho-Olivares remained steadfast in her commitment to journalism as a tool for social change. She continued to expose the truth and give voice to the oppressed, even when it came at great personal cost. Her unwavering resolve and unrelenting pursuit of justice made her a symbol of press freedom and a beacon of hope for those who sought accountability during the dark days of martial law.
In addition to her courageous journalism, Cacho-Olivares also played a crucial role in shaping the media landscape in the Philippines. She co-founded the Daily Tribune, an independent newspaper known for its critical reporting and unwavering commitment to press freedom. As the editor-in-chief of the Daily Tribune, she continued to be a vocal critic of abuses of power and corruption, and her newspaper became a platform for voices that were often silenced by those in authority.

Ongpin Awards for Excellence in Journalism, and was widely regarded as a trailblazer and role model for aspiring journalists in the Philippines and beyond.
