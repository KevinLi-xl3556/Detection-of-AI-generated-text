The Oscars are the biggest night in Hollywood, where the best and brightest of the film industry gather to celebrate their achievements. The 2020 Oscars promise to be an exciting event, with some of the best movies of the year in contention for awards. In this article, we’ll take a look at what to expect from the 2020 Oscar nominations.
Best Picture
The Best Picture category is always the most highly anticipated at the Oscars, and this year is no exception. There are some strong contenders in the running, including “Joker,” “1917,” and “Once Upon a Time in Hollywood.” “Joker” is a dark and powerful film that explores the origins of one of the most iconic villains in comic book history. “1917” is a war movie that takes a unique and innovative approach to the genre, while “Once Upon a Time in Hollywood” is a love letter to 1960s Hollywood, with a star-studded cast that includes Leonardo DiCaprio and Brad Pitt.
Best Director
The Best Director category is always a closely watched one, as it recognizes the vision and talent of the people behind the camera. This year, there are some strong contenders, including Sam Mendes for “1917” and Martin Scorsese for “The Irishman.” Mendes’ direction of “1917” has been widely praised for its innovative approach, while Scorsese’s work on “The Irishman” is seen as a masterful display of his skills as a filmmaker.
Best Actor
The Best Actor category is always hotly contested, and this year is no exception. Joaquin Phoenix is a frontrunner for his performance in “Joker,” which has been hailed as one of the best of his career. Adam Driver is also in contention for his role in “Marriage Story,” as is Leonardo DiCaprio for his turn in “Once Upon a Time in Hollywood.”
Best Actress
The Best Actress category is always a closely watched one, and this year is no exception. Scarlett Johansson is in the running for her performance in “Marriage Story,” as is Renee Zellweger for her portrayal of Judy Garland in “Judy.” Saoirse Ronan is also in contention for her role in “Little Women,” while Charlize Theron is being considered for her portrayal of Megyn Kelly in “Bombshell.”
Overall, the 2020 Oscar nominations promise to be an exciting and closely contested event. With so many talented actors and filmmakers in the running, it’s sure to be a night to remember.
