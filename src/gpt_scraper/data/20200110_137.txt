Chhapaak Review: Life and Activism After an Acid Attack
Chhapaak is a 2020 Indian Hindi-language drama film directed by Meghna Gulzar and produced by Deepika Padukone and Meghna Gulzar. The movie is based on the real-life story of acid attack survivor Laxmi Agarwal. The film portrays the struggles of Malti, played by Deepika Padukone, who is attacked with acid and her journey towards fighting for justice, overcoming trauma, and finally coming to terms with her physical and emotional scars.
The film begins with Malti, a young woman who aspires to be a singer, being attacked by Bashir Khan, a man who was obsessed with her. The acid attack leaves her disfigured and traumatized. The film then follows her journey as she tries to rebuild her life and overcome the hurdles that come with being an acid attack survivor.
Deepika Padukone delivers a stunning performance as Malti, capturing the character’s pain, resilience, and courage. The film does an excellent job of highlighting the physical and emotional trauma that acid attack survivors face. The makeup and prosthetics used to depict Malti’s scars are incredibly realistic, making the film all the more poignant.
Apart from Malti’s personal story, the film also addresses the larger issue of acid attacks and the need for stricter laws and regulations to prevent such incidents. The film raises awareness about acid violence and its devastating consequences on the victim’s life.
Chhapaak also showcases the power of activism and the impact it can have on society. Malti’s fight for justice and her efforts towards raising awareness about acid attacks and supporting other survivors are an inspiration to all.
The film is not just about Malti’s story but also about the support she receives from her family and friends. The character of Amol, played by Vikrant Massey, is particularly significant. He is a journalist who helps Malti with her case and becomes a close friend and confidante. His character highlights the importance of support systems for survivors and the role that society can play in bringing about change.
Overall, Chhapaak is a powerful and moving film that highlights the resilience of the human spirit. It is a must-watch for anyone interested in social issues and the power of activism. The film leaves a lasting impact and serves as a reminder of the need for compassion and empathy towards survivors of violence.
