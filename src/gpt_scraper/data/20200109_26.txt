If you’re a classical music lover living in or visiting New York City this weekend, you’re in for a real treat. The city boasts a rich cultural scene, and its classical music offerings are no exception. Whether you’re in the mood for something grand and majestic or intimate and subtle, there’s a classical music concert happening this weekend that’s sure to tickle your fancy. Here are six classical music concerts that you won’t want to miss:


New York Philharmonic: Beethoven’s Symphony No. 9
Friday, 7:30pm, David Geffen Hall
The New York Philharmonic is one of the world’s most prestigious symphony orchestras, and they’re kicking off the weekend with a performance of Beethoven’s Symphony No. 9, which features the famous “Ode to Joy” choral section. This performance will be led by renowned conductor Jaap van Zweden and will feature soloists Ying Fang, Christa Mayer, and Russell Thomas, as well as the New York Choral Artists.


The Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center: Dvorak and Tchaikovsky
Friday, 7:30pm, Alice Tully Hall
For something a bit more intimate, head to Alice Tully Hall to catch the Chamber Music Society of Lincoln Center’s performance of works by Dvorak and Tchaikovsky. The program includes Tchaikovsky’s Souvenir de Florence and Dvorak’s Piano Quintet in A major, Op. 81. This concert features a lineup of some of the most talented chamber musicians in the world.


Metropolitan Opera: La Boheme
Saturday, 1:00pm, Metropolitan Opera House
If opera is more your style, you won’t want to miss the Metropolitan Opera’s performance of Puccini’s La Boheme. This classic opera tells the story of a group of bohemian artists living in 19th century Paris and features some of the most beautiful and memorable music in the opera repertoire. This performance stars Ailyn Perez and Atalla Ayan as Mimi and Rodolfo, respectively.


The New York Virtuoso Singers: American Voices
Saturday, 7:30pm, Church of St. Mary the Virgin
For a celebration of American music, check out The New York Virtuoso Singers’ performance of American Voices at the Church of St. Mary the Virgin. This concert features works by composers such as Aaron Copland, Samuel Barber, and Leonard Bernstein, and showcases some of the most talented choral singers in the city.


The Juilliard School: Symphony Orchestra
Sunday, 3:00pm, Alice Tully Hall
The Juilliard School is one of the most prestigious music conservatories in the world, and you can catch their Symphony Orchestra in action this weekend at Alice Tully Hall. Led by conductor Jeffrey Milarsky, the program includes works by Stravinsky, Adams, and Berlioz, and features Juilliard’s most talented young musicians.


Orpheus Chamber Orchestra: Haydn and Mozart
Sunday, 5:00pm, Carnegie Hall
For a grand finale to your weekend of classical music, head to Carnegie Hall to see the Orpheus Chamber Orchestra’s performance of Haydn’s Symphony No. 103 and Mozart’s Piano Concerto No. 20. This concert features renowned pianist Fazil Say and promises to be an unforgettable evening of music.


No matter what your taste in classical music, there’s a concert happening in New York City this weekend that’s sure to delight and inspire you. So grab your tickets and get ready to be transported by the power of music.
