11 New Books We Recommend This Week
There’s nothing quite like curling up with a good book, and this week we’ve got a great list of new releases to add to your reading list. From heartwarming memoirs to thrilling mysteries, here are 11 new books we recommend this week.


“The Road to Unfreedom” by Timothy Snyder - This powerful political history examines the rise of authoritarianism in America and Europe.


“Dear Senthuran” by Akwaeke Emezi - A candid and introspective memoir about gender, race, and identity.


“The Bomber Mafia” by Malcolm Gladwell - The bestselling author takes a deep dive into the strategies and moral dilemmas faced by the Air Force during World War II.


“What Strange Paradise” by Omar El Akkad - A gripping and thought-provoking novel about a Syrian refugee boy who finds himself stranded on a Greek island.


“Jackpot” by Nic Stone - A heartwarming YA novel about two teens who win the lottery and must navigate newfound wealth and responsibility.


“The Last Thing He Told Me” by Laura Dave - A twisty and suspenseful thriller about a woman who discovers her husband has been keeping secrets.


“The Anthropocene Reviewed” by John Green - The beloved YA author and podcaster reflects on the human impact on the environment through a series of essays.


“The President’s Daughter” by Bill Clinton and James Patterson - A high-octane political thriller about a former president whose daughter is kidnapped by terrorists.


“The Guncle” by Steven Rowley - A heartwarming and funny novel about a gay uncle who becomes the caretaker for his young niece and nephew.


“Ace of Spades” by Faridah Àbíké-Íyímídé - A gripping YA novel about two students who must uncover the identity of a mysterious school-wide bully.


“The Hidden Palace” by Helene Wecker - The much-anticipated sequel to “The Golem and the Jinni” continues the story of two mythical creatures navigating life in turn-of-the-century New York City.


No matter what your reading preferences are, there’s something for everyone on this list of 11 new books we recommend this week. So go ahead, pick up a book, and get lost in a good story.
