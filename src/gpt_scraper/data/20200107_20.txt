T’s Favorite Nourishing Recipes for a More Healthful 2020
With the start of a new year, many of us are looking to make healthier choices when it comes to what we eat. Eating a well-balanced, nutrient-dense diet is crucial to maintaining good health and preventing chronic disease. But sometimes, it can be challenging to know where to start, especially if you’re used to eating a lot of processed or convenience foods.
That’s where T’s favorite nourishing recipes come in. These recipes are packed with wholesome, nutrient-dense ingredients that will help you feel your best in 2020. Whether you’re looking for breakfast, lunch, dinner, or a snack, these recipes are sure to satisfy your taste buds while nourishing your body.

Blueberry Banana Smoothie Bowl

Smoothie bowls are a great way to pack in a ton of nutrients in one meal. This blueberry banana smoothie bowl is no exception. It’s made with frozen blueberries, bananas, almond milk, and protein powder, and topped with sliced banana, fresh blueberries, and granola. This smoothie bowl is high in fiber, antioxidants, and protein, making it the perfect breakfast to start your day.

Quinoa Salad with Roasted Vegetables

Quinoa is a great source of protein and fiber, making it a great base for a nourishing salad. This quinoa salad is loaded with roasted vegetables, including sweet potatoes, Brussels sprouts, and red onion, and dressed with a tangy balsamic vinaigrette. This salad is perfect for a light lunch or dinner and will keep you feeling full and satisfied.

Sweet Potato and Black Bean Tacos

Tacos are always a crowd-pleaser, and these sweet potato and black bean tacos are no exception. The sweet potatoes are roasted until tender and tossed with black beans, lime juice, and cilantro. The tacos are then topped with avocado, salsa, and a dollop of Greek yogurt. These tacos are high in fiber, protein, and healthy fats, making them a great choice for a meatless dinner.

Oven-Baked Salmon with Lemon and Dill

Salmon is one of the healthiest foods you can eat, packed with omega-3 fatty acids, protein, and vitamin D. This oven-baked salmon with lemon and dill is a simple and delicious way to incorporate more of this superfood into your diet. The salmon is seasoned with lemon, dill, and garlic, then baked in the oven until tender and flaky. Serve it with a side of roasted vegetables for a complete meal.

Chocolate Chia Seed Pudding

Just because you’re eating healthily doesn’t mean you can’t indulge in a sweet treat now and then. This chocolate chia seed pudding is the perfect guilt-free dessert. It’s made with chia seeds, almond milk, cocoa powder, and a touch of maple syrup. The chia seeds are high in fiber and omega-3 fatty acids, making this pudding not only delicious but also nutritious.
In conclusion, eating a well-balanced, nutrient-dense diet is crucial to good health. These nourishing recipes are a great place to start if you’re looking to make healthier choices in 2020. With a little bit of planning and preparation, you can enjoy delicious, satisfying meals that will keep you feeling your best.
