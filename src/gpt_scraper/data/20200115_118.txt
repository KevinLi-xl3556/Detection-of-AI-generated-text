Mark Hamill, an iconic figure in the Star Wars franchise, has set a new Guinness World Record for having the most autographs given by a single signer in three hours.
Hamill, who played the iconic role of Luke Skywalker in the original Star Wars trilogy, surpassed his own record, which he had previously set in 2017.
The event, which took place at Star Wars Celebration in Chicago, saw Hamill sign a staggering 5,000 autographs in the allotted time, beating his previous record of 4,600.
Fans from all over the world lined up for hours to meet the legendary actor and get their hands on an autograph. The atmosphere was electric, with fans dressed in costumes, waving light sabers, and cheering as Hamill signed their beloved Star Wars merchandise.
Hamill expressed his appreciation for his fans, saying, “I wouldn’t have a career if it wasn’t for them. They’re the reason I’m here, and I’m so grateful to be able to give back to them in this way.”
The record-breaking attempt was not just a personal achievement for Hamill. It was also a chance to raise money for The Starlight Children’s Foundation, a charity that provides support for seriously ill children and their families.
Hamill’s dedication to charity work is nothing new. He has been a long-time supporter of various charities, including Make-A-Wish Foundation, St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital, and many more.
In addition to breaking records and giving back to the community, Hamill has continued to be a vital part of the Star Wars franchise in recent years. He reprised his role as Luke Skywalker in The Last Jedi and The Rise of Skywalker, earning critical acclaim for his performances.
With his enduring passion for his craft and his unwavering dedication to his fans and charitable causes, Mark Hamill remains an inspirational figure for Star Wars fans and beyond. His latest Guinness World Record achievement is a testament to his enduring appeal and the impact he has had on the industry and his fans’ lives.
