The New York Public Library is one of the largest libraries in the world with over 53 million items within its collection, ranging from books to manuscripts, photographs, maps, and more. As a result, the library is a treasure trove for book lovers, researchers, and scholars.
Ever wondered what books were the most checked-out in the history of the library? Thanks to data from the library, we now have a list of the top ten most borrowed books of all time.

“The Snowy Day” - by Ezra Jack Keats

First published in 1962, “The Snowy Day” has captivated generations of readers with its simple, poetic prose and striking illustrations.  The timeless tale of a little boy named Peter taking a winter walk and experiencing the magic of a snowy day has been borrowed over 485,583 times making it the most checked-out book in the history of the New York Public Library.

“The Cat in the Hat” - by Dr. Seuss

Another timeless classic, “The Cat in the Hat” has been a favorite among readers of all ages since its publication in 1957. The book’s wacky rhymes, lovable characters, and colorful illustrations have made it a favorite among children and adults alike. It has been borrowed over 469,650 times.

“1984” - by George Orwell

Since its publication in 1949, “1984” has been a staple of dystopian literature. The novel has been borrowed over 441,770 times and continues to be a relevant and thought-provoking classic.

“Where the Wild Things Are” - by Maurice Sendak

This 1963 children’s book tells the story of a young boy named Max who sails off to a fantastical world full of “wild things.” The book has been borrowed over 436,016 times.

“To Kill a Mockingbird” - by Harper Lee

Harper Lee’s Pulitzer Prize-winning novel is a poignant and powerful tale about racial inequality and injustice. The book has been borrowed over 422,912 times.

“Charlotte’s Web” - by E.B. White

This charming story of a spider named Charlotte and her friendship with a pig named Wilbur has touched the hearts of readers young and old since its publication in 1952. It has been borrowed over 337,948 times.

“Fahrenheit 451” - by Ray Bradbury

Ray Bradbury’s chilling tale of a society where books are banned and “firemen” burn any that are found has become a literary classic. The book has been borrowed over 316,404 times.

“How to Win Friends and Influence People” - by Dale Carnegie

First published in 1936, this self-help book has sold millions of copies worldwide and has been borrowed over 284,524 times from the New York Public Library. The book has been praised for its timeless advice and practical techniques for building relationships and achieving success.

“Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone” - by J.K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling’s enchanting tale of a young boy named Harry Potter and his adventures at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry has captured the hearts of readers around the world. It has been borrowed over 231,022 times.

“The Very Hungry Caterpillar” - by Eric Carle

This beloved children’s book has been charming readers since its publication in 1969. The book’s colorful illustrations and simple but playful text has made it a fixture in classrooms and libraries across the globe. It has been borrowed over 189,550 times.
In conclusion, these ten books have not only been popular with readers within the walls of the New York Public Library but have also influenced and entertained millions of people around the world. The timeless themes and stories within these books continue to resonate with readers of all ages, and their popularity only seems to grow with each passing year.
