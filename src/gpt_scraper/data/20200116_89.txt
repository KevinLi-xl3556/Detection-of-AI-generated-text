Martin Luther King Jr. Day: 8 Places in New York to Remember His Legacy
Martin Luther King Jr. Day, celebrated on the third Monday of January every year, is a national holiday in the United States. This holiday honors and remembers the life and legacy of Martin Luther King Jr., an American Baptist minister, activist, and leader in the civil rights movement in the 1950s and 1960s. He is best known for his role in the advancement of civil rights using nonviolent civil disobedience based on his Christian beliefs. In commemoration of this day, here are 8 places to visit in New York to remember the legacy of Martin Luther King Jr.


The Martin Luther King Jr. Memorial Park in Manhattan, located at 113th St. and Manhattan Ave. This park was named after Martin Luther King Jr. shortly after his assassination in 1968 and features a granite bust of him.


Martin Luther King Jr. Educational Campus, located in the Mott Haven section of the Bronx, is a high school named after him. The campus features a mural of Martin Luther King Jr. painted by students and staff in 2018.


The New York Public Library’s Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture, located in Harlem, is a research library and archive devoted to the study of the global black experience. The Schomburg Center houses important collections and exhibitions about Martin Luther King Jr., including rare photographs, manuscripts, and publications.


The Riverside Church, located in Morningside Heights, Manhattan, is where Martin Luther King Jr. delivered his famous Beyond Vietnam: A Time to Break Silence speech on April 4, 1967. The church honors his legacy by holding events and services on Martin Luther King Jr. Day.


The Malcolm X and Dr. Betty Shabazz Memorial and Education Center, located in Washington Heights, Manhattan, is a museum honoring the lives and legacies of Malcolm X and his wife Betty Shabazz, a civil rights activist and educator. The museum features exhibitions about the civil rights movement, including the work of Martin Luther King Jr.


The National Museum of African American History and Culture, located in Washington D.C., is part of the Smithsonian Institution and is dedicated to the documentation of African American life, history, and culture. The museum houses a collection of thousands of artifacts related to the civil rights movement, including items related to Martin Luther King Jr.


The Brooklyn Academy of Music, or BAM, located in Brooklyn, is a performing arts center that has hosted performances about the civil rights movement. Martin Luther King Jr.'s speeches and writings have been incorporated into some of these performances.


The Historic Children’s Museum, located in Brooklyn, has an exhibit about the civil rights movement that includes a section on Martin Luther King Jr. The exhibit features interactive activities, exhibits, and events that help visitors understand the history and impact of the civil rights movement.


In conclusion, New York offers a variety of ways to remember and honor Martin Luther King Jr.'s legacy on his national holiday. These eight places offer a glimpse into the civil rights movement and the impact that Martin Luther King Jr. had on history.
