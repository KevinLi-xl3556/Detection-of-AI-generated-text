If you’re someone who loves to cook, finding recipes that you enjoy making and that turn out well can be a game-changer. Instead of constantly searching for new recipes to try, you can put some tried-and-true recipes on repeat. These recipes will become staples in your cooking repertoire and make meal planning a breeze. Here are a few recipes that are worth putting on repeat.

Roasted Vegetables

Roasted vegetables are a simple, healthy, and delicious side dish that can be made with almost any vegetable. All you need to do is chop up your favorite vegetables (think carrots, broccoli, cauliflower, bell peppers, and onions), toss them in olive oil, sprinkle with salt and pepper, and roast them in the oven until they’re golden and tender. Roasted vegetables are perfect for meal prep, as they can be stored in the fridge for up to a week.

One-Pot Pasta

One-pot pasta is the ultimate lazy weeknight meal. All you need is one pot and about 20 minutes to make a delicious, comforting pasta dish. Simply combine pasta, canned tomatoes, garlic, onion, and your choice of protein (like chicken or shrimp) in a pot and let it simmer until the pasta is cooked through. One-pot pasta is easy to customize, so you can add your favorite veggies or spices to make it your own.

Overnight Oats

Overnight oats are a great make-ahead breakfast option that can be customized to your liking. Simply combine rolled oats, milk, and your choice of sweetener (like honey or maple syrup) in a jar and let it sit in the fridge overnight. In the morning, you can top your oats with fresh fruit, nuts, or seeds for added flavor and nutrition. Overnight oats are perfect for busy mornings when you don’t have time to make breakfast from scratch.

Stir-Fry

Stir-fry is a versatile and healthy dinner option that can be made with any protein and vegetables you have on hand. Simply cook your protein (like chicken or tofu) in a hot pan with garlic and ginger, add your veggies (like broccoli, snap peas, or bell peppers), and stir-fry until everything is cooked through. Serve over rice or noodles for a filling and flavorful meal.

Smoothie Bowl

Smoothie bowls are a healthy and refreshing breakfast or snack option that are easy to make and customize. Simply blend frozen fruit, milk or yogurt, and your choice of protein powder or nut butter until smooth, and then top with your favorite toppings (like granola, fresh fruit, or coconut flakes). Smoothie bowls are a great way to get in a serving of fruits and veggies in a delicious and satisfying way.
By putting these recipes on repeat, you’ll have a handful of go-to meals that you know you’ll enjoy and that are easy to make. Whether you’re cooking for yourself or your family, these recipes are sure to become staples in your kitchen.
