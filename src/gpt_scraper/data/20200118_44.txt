7 Great Contemporary Novels for Teenagers
As a teenager, reading is an excellent way to escape from the woes of everyday life and delve into a world of imagination, emotion, and excitement. With numerous books out there, it can be hard to decide which ones to dive into. That’s why we’ve compiled a list of seven great contemporary novels for teenagers that are sure to keep you entertained.


The Hate U Give by Angie Thomas – A poignant and timely novel about race and police brutality, The Hate U Give tells the story of a 16-year-old girl named Starr who witnesses a police officer shoot her unarmed best friend. The novel explores themes of racial identity, societal injustice, and the power of words and actions.


Turtles All The Way Down by John Green – A beautiful and honest look at mental illness, Turtles All the Way Down follows a teenage girl named Aza Holmes who struggles with obsessive-compulsive disorder. The novel is a raw portrayal of Aza’s inward battle with her own mind and the people around her.


The Fault in Our Stars by John Green – A touching and heart-wrenching tale of two teenagers who fall in love while battling cancer. The Fault in Our Stars explores the beauty and fragility of life while reminding readers to embrace the now.


All the Bright Places by Jennifer Niven – A story about mental illness and teenage love, All the Bright Places follows two teenagers, Theodore and Violet, who are brought together by a school project. As they discover each other and themselves, they must also navigate their own personal demons.


The Maze Runner by James Dashner – A thrilling dystopian novel about a group of teenage boys trapped in a maze with no memory of their past lives. The Maze Runner is a fast-paced adventure that keeps readers on their toes.


Eleanor & Park by Rainbow Rowell – A beautiful and unconventional love story set in 1986 between two misfits, Eleanor and Park. Though their love is complicated by social and familial factors, their bond is unwavering and touching.


A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Maas – A thrilling adventure novel about a young woman named Feyre who is taken captive by a faerie prince. The novel is a wonderful blend of action, adventure, and romance that will keep readers captivated.


In conclusion, these seven great contemporary novels for teenagers offer different themes, genres, and journeys, but each one is a worthwhile read that will capture your heart and your imagination. So, go ahead and choose the ones that seem most interesting to you and get lost in the world of words. Happy reading!
