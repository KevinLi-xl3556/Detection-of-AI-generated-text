Ed Filipowski, 58, Pioneer of Fashion Public Relations, Dies
The fashion industry is mourning the loss of one of its most respected figures, Ed Filipowski, who passed away at the age of 58. Filipowski, who was the co-chairman of the fashion public relations firm KCD, had a long and illustrious career that spanned over three decades. He was known for his passion, creativity, and unwavering dedication to his craft, and his contributions to the fashion industry will be deeply missed.
Filipowski was born in Edinburgh, Scotland, in 1964, but grew up in Canada. He began his career in fashion as a fashion editor at the Montreal Gazette before moving to New York City in the mid-1980s. He quickly made a name for himself as a public relations executive, working for several high-profile fashion brands, including Giorgio Armani and Calvin Klein.
In 1989, Filipowski joined forces with fellow PR executive Kezia Keeble to start their own agency, KCD. The agency quickly became a force to be reckoned with in the fashion industry, representing some of the biggest names in fashion, including Marc Jacobs, Tom Ford, and Alexander McQueen. Filipowski was known for his impeccable taste and his ability to spot emerging fashion talent, and he played a key role in launching the careers of many of today’s top designers.
Filipowski was also an early adopter of technology in the fashion industry, recognizing its potential to revolutionize the way brands communicate with their audiences. Under his leadership, KCD was at the forefront of using digital media and social media to promote its clients, and Filipowski was a tireless advocate for the power of storytelling in fashion.
In addition to his work at KCD, Filipowski was also a beloved figure in the fashion community. He was known for his kindness, his generosity, and his willingness to help young talent. He was also a mentor to many, and his influence can be seen in the work of countless fashion professionals who had the privilege of working with him over the years.
News of Filipowski’s passing has been met with an outpouring of grief and tributes from across the fashion industry. Many have taken to social media to express their condolences and share memories of working with Filipowski. In a statement, KCD described him as “an inspiring leader, mentor, and friend” and said that his “impact on the fashion industry is immeasurable.”
Filipowski’s passing is a tremendous loss for the fashion industry, but his legacy will live on through the countless designers, publicists, and fashion professionals he inspired and mentored over the years.
