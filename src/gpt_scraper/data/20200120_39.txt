Extradition Hearings Begin for Meng Wanzhou, Huawei Officer Held in Canada
The highly anticipated extradition hearings for Meng Wanzhou, the chief financial officer of Huawei Technologies Co. Ltd. has begun in Canada. The case has become a major point of contention between the United States and China, with both sides arguing vehemently about the legal and political implications of the case. Meng’s lawyers have argued that the charges against her are politically motivated and that her arrest was unlawful. They have also accused Canadian authorities of illegally searching Meng’s electronic devices without her consent, a claim which the Canadian government has denied.
The hearings, which are being held in a British Columbia courthouse, are expected to last several months as both sides present their evidence and arguments. The outcome of the case could have major implications for the future of U.S.-China relations, as well as for the global technology industry.
