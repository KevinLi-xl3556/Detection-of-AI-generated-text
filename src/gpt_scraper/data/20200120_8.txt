David Olney, a highly respected singer-songwriter, passed away suddenly on Saturday, January 18th while performing on stage in Florida. He was 71 years old.

Born in Providence, Rhode Island in 1948, Olney began his musical career in the 1960s. He moved to Nashville in the early 1970s, where he quickly became a prominent figure in the city’s music scene. Over the years, he released over twenty albums, including “Deeper Well,” “High, Wide, and Lonesome,” and “This Side or the Other.”
Olney was known for his distinctive voice, which ranged from the soft and tender to the raw and gritty. His lyrics were often introspective, and dealt with themes of love, loss, and the human condition. He wrote songs for a wide variety of artists, including Emmylou Harris, Linda Ronstadt, and Steve Earle.
