11 New Books We Recommend This Week
Reading is one of life’s greatest pleasures, and with so many books being published every week, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed by choice. That’s why we’ve gone ahead and rounded up 11 new books that we recommend you read this week.


The Silent Patient by Alex Michaelides: This book is a thriller that will keep you on the edge of your seat from start to finish. It tells the story of a woman who is accused of murdering her husband but refuses to speak. A psychiatrist attempts to uncover the truth behind her silence in this gripping page-turner.


The Final Revival of Opal & Nev by Dawnie Walton: This novel is set in the '70s and tells the story of a legendary rock duo. It explores themes of race, identity, and the power of music to bring people together.


Ace of Spades by Faridah Abike-Iyo: This YA thriller follows two students at an elite private school who work together to uncover a mysterious anonymous figure who is revealing their classmates’ darkest secrets.


The Plot by Jean Hanff Korelitz: This is a thriller about a struggling writer who steals a plot from a deceased student. The book explores themes of ambition, morality, and the dark side of the publishing industry.


The Anthropocene Reviewed by John Green: This nonfiction book is a collection of essays about the Anthropocene era, which is marked by human impact on the planet. It covers a wide range of topics, from rock formations to Diet Dr. Pepper, and offers insights into human nature and our relationship with the world around us.


The Donut King by Ted Ngoy: This memoir tells the story of Ted Ngoy, a Cambodian refugee who arrived in the US with nothing and went on to become the “Donut King” of California. The book explores themes of immigration, entrepreneurship, and the American Dream.


Mister Impossible by Maggie Stiefvater: This is the second book in the Dreamer Trilogy, which follows a group of young adults as they navigate the dream world and grapple with the consequences of their actions. Mysteries deepen and tensions rise in this thrilling sequel.


The Plot Thickens: A Memoir by Mary McNamara: This memoir is written by an Emmy-winning television critic who explores the intersection of her professional and personal life. It’s a witty, insightful look at the entertainment industry and what it means to be a critic in the age of social media.


Heartbreak Bay by Rachel Caine: This is the fifth book in the Stillhouse Lake series, which follows a former housewife who becomes a vigilante after discovering her husband’s dark secret. In this installment, she returns to her hometown to confront her past and solve a new mystery.


The Maidens by Alex Michaelides: This is another thriller from Alex Michaelides, the author of The Silent Patient. It follows a group of Cambridge students who are targeted by a serial killer, and it will keep you guessing until the very end.


The One Hundred Years of Lenni and Margot by Marianne Cronin: This novel follows the friendship between 17-year-old Lenni and 83-year-old Margot, who meet in a hospital and begin creating an art project to celebrate their lives. It’s a moving exploration of life, love, and loss.


So there you have it, our top picks for 11 new books to read this week. Whether you’re in the mood for a thriller, a memoir, or a heartwarming novel, there’s something on this list for everyone. Happy reading!
