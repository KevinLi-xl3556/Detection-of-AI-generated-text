As the weekend approaches, many of us are faced with the daunting task of deciding what to cook for family and friends. With so many food options available, it can be difficult to settle on the perfect meal that will satisfy everyone's appetite. However, The New York Times has got you covered with some of the best recipes for the weekend.

First up, we have a mouth-watering recipe for grilled chicken marinated in a spicy yogurt sauce. This Indian-inspired dish is bursting with flavors of ginger, garlic, and cumin, and is perfect for those hot summer evenings. Serve with a side of roasted vegetables and a cooling cucumber and mint raita to complete the meal.

If you're in the mood for something more indulgent, try making homemade pizza from scratch. With a crispy crust and topped with your favorite ingredients, such as mozzarella, fresh basil, and juicy tomatoes, this recipe is sure to satisfy your cravings. Bonus points for adding some garlic knots or cheesy breadsticks to the mix.

For a vegetarian option, fill up on a hearty lentil and vegetable soup. The combination of lentils, carrots, and celery creates a rich and satisfying broth, while zucchini and spinach add a fresh twist to this classic dish. Serve with some crusty bread and butter for the ultimate comfort meal.

If you're feeling adventurous, why not try your hand at making sushi rolls at home? From salmon and avocado to crispy tempura shrimp, the possibilities are endless. With a bit of practice, you can master the art of rolling sushi and impress your guests with this sophisticated and delicious meal.

Finally, for a sweet treat, bake a batch of chocolate chip cookies. These classic cookies are the perfect comfort food and are loved by both kids and adults alike. Serve them warm with a cold glass of milk for the ultimate indulgence.

In conclusion, whether you're in the mood for something spicy, indulgent, or comforting, there's a dish for everyone to enjoy this weekend. So head to the kitchen, grab your apron, and get cooking with these delicious recipes from The New York Times.
