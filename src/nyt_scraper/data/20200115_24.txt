In her 2008 essay, “Take Me as I Am, Whoever I Am,” Terri Cheney writes about her lifelong battle with bipolar disorder, a debilitating mental illness which she had struggled to keep secret from her lovers and co-workers.
Ms. Cheney’s witty and moving essay inspired one of the episodes in the Modern Love Amazon Prime Video series. This week, the Modern Love podcast revisits Rebecca Hall’s reading of Ms. Cheney’s essay.
Ms. Hall is an actress known for her roles in the movies “Vicky Cristina Barcelona” and “Christine.” Ms. Cheney is the author of the memoirs “Manic” and “The Dark Side of Innocence: Growing Up Bipolar.” Stay tuned after the reading to hear more from her, Ms. Hall and the Modern Love editor Daniel Jones.
