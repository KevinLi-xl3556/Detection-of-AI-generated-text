Is It Offensive for Sports Teams and Their Fans to Use Native American Names, Imagery and Gestures?
Native American names and symbols have long been used by all types of American sports teams at the professional, college and high school levels, such as the Cleveland Indians, Atlanta Braves and Washington Redskins. But facing pressure and protest, many teams are now changing their nicknames and mascots.
This Sunday, the Kansas City Chiefs will face the San Francisco 49ers for Super Bowl LIV. It is likely that at some point during the game, Chiefs fans will begin their favored cheer: the tomahawk chop.
